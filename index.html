Our Story

About Union of Genius

Founded in 2019 by a coalition of tech professionals, educators, and development workers, Union of Genius was born from a single belief: that Nigeria's greatest resource is its people, and its greatest multiplier is technology.

Our Mission

Why We Exist

Nigeria has one of the world's youngest and largest populations. By 2050, 1 in 5 Africans will be Nigerian. If these young people are equipped with digital skills, Nigeria will not just compete with the world — it will lead it.

Yet today, millions of Nigerians lack access to basic digital literacy. The infrastructure gap, the policy gap, and the knowledge gap conspire to hold back a generation of potential. Union of Genius is determined to close these gaps.

We operate at the intersection of education, advocacy, and community — because change at scale requires all three.

Our Vision

Nigeria as Africa's Digital Capital

"Every Nigerian deserves access to the tools of the digital age."
— Union of Genius Founding Charter, 2019

We envision a Nigeria where geography, gender, and economic background no longer determine one's access to technology — where every Nigerian can participate in, contribute to, and benefit from the digital economy.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

01

Inclusion

Technology must work for everyone. We deliberately design our programmes to reach the most marginalised — rural communities, women, persons with disabilities, and youth in conflict zones.

02

Excellence

We hold our programmes to the highest standards. Our bootcamps, curricula, and advocacy materials are peer-reviewed, internationally benchmarked, and continuously improved.

03

Transparency

As stewards of donor funds and public trust, we publish our accounts, impact reports, and programme evaluations openly and annually.

04

Sustainability

We design programmes that outlive our direct involvement — training local trainers, building community institutions, and advocating for policies that make tech access permanent.

Our Journey
2019

Foundation

Union of Genius founded in Lagos by a group of 12 tech professionals and educators. First programme — a 6-week coding workshop for 40 youth in Surulere.

2020

Digital Pivot

COVID-19 forces our programmes online. We reach 1,200 learners across 18 states — more than triple our previous reach.

2021

Girls in Tech Launch

Girls in Tech Nigeria launches with 300 founding scholars. Partnerships with Google.org and Ford Foundation announced.

2022

Policy Breakthrough

Union of Genius co-authors Nigeria's National Digital Literacy Framework, adopted by the Federal Ministry of Education.

2023

12,000 Trained

We cross 10,000 alumni and open our first physical tech hub — the Genius Hub — in Abuja's Garki district.

2024–25

Northern Expansion

Expanding into all 19 northern states with dedicated Arabic-language digital literacy content and mobile training units.
